About

Operating at the boundary

I’m an Electrical Engineering and Physics student at the University of Massachusetts Boston. My work sits where theory, code, and hardware collide: signal processing, embedded systems, wireless links, and intelligent control.

I care about systems that can be measured, reproduced, and scaled. That means testbeds instead of slides, data instead of claims, and code + schematics that actually compile and run on real hardware.

Research

UCaN Lab · wireless futures

At the UCaN Lab, I work on next-generation connectivity in ultra-dense wireless environments, with a focus on hybrid Radio Frequency / Optical Wireless Communication (RF/OWC) and mobility-aware testbeds.

Focus Areas

My contributions live at the edge of communications theory and experimental validation.

  • Hybrid RF/Optical wireless links
  • Ultra-dense networking scenarios
  • Motion-capture-driven mobility analysis
  • Raspberry Pi sensing and RSSI logging

Key Contributions

  • Integrated the OpenBuilds ACRO 2020 gantry with OptiTrack to create a precise, reproducible mobility evaluation platform.
  • Helped implement a hybrid RF/OWC testbed to study link behavior under movement and interference.
  • Developed Raspberry Pi 5 receiver units for continuous RSSI capture and experiment logging.
